The Infinite Loop: Meme Aesthetics & Early Web Absurdity

Exhibition curated by Andrew C.

Net art is art made for and experienced on the internet. While many net art projects focus on interactivity or complex ideas, some works use humor, repetition, and online aesthetics to create artistic experiences. "Online aesthetics" refers to the visual culture dictated by the technical limitations of the early web—think low-fidelity pixel art, high-contrast digital colors, and small file sizes that forced animations into tight, endless loops. Three famous examples of this meme-style net art are Nyan Cat, Hamster Dance, and Badger Badger Badger.

All three were created specifically for the web, meaning their medium is entirely essential to the work. A defining strategy of this era was the internet's fascination with dancing characters. Stripped of traditional plotlines or linear narratives, these dancing loops reduce the art to pure visual and auditory rhythm. This repetition is part of the art—it highlights the chaotic, hypnotic, and playful nature of early online culture. Similar to the rise of electronic and techno music loops during the same era, these infinite dances offer a trancelike, absurd experience that provides a sense of comfort or hyper-focus against a barrage of information. They share a low-fidelity, messy aesthetic that reflects early internet humor and makes them fun to share and remix.

Humor and absurdity are central to these works. The enjoyment comes from the ridiculous, endless animations and music, which also comment on the repetitive and attention-grabbing nature of internet content. While they may not be interactive like traditional net art, they engage viewers through sharing, remixing, and repeated viewing.

Nyan Cat

Christopher Torres | 2011
A pixelated Pop-Tart cat flies endlessly through space to a looping chiptune song. This whimsical animation turns simple repetition and absurdity into an iconic piece of internet-born net art.

Hamster Dance

Deidre LaCarte | 1997
Rows of animated hamsters dance to a high-speed, looping song. Its chaotic visuals and hypnotic repetition highlight the playful creativity of early internet culture.

Badger Badger Badger

Jonti “Weebl” Picking | 2003
A Flash animation of dancing badgers, mushrooms, and a snake repeats endlessly with a catchy chant. This absurd, looping experience exemplifies meme-style net art and web-native humor.
